Secure

One of the most important aspects of Apple Pay is its security. Apple Pay uses tokenization to protect users' financial information. This means that when you add a credit or debit card to your Apple Pay account, Apple creates a unique token that is used to represent your card number. This token is stored on your device and is never shared with merchants or other third parties. When you make a payment with Apple Pay, the token is used instead of your actual card number. This helps to protect your financial information from being stolen or compromised.

Tokenization is a very effective way to protect users' financial information. It is used by many other payment methods, such as credit cards and debit cards.
