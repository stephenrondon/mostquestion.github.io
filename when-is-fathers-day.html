When Will Father's Day Be This Year?

Father's Day is a holiday honoring fathers and paternal figures, celebrated on various days in many countries. It complements Mother's Day, which is dedicated to mothers.

The day originated in the United States, where it was first celebrated in the early 20th century. Sonora Dodd, a woman from Washington, proposed the idea after hearing a sermon about Mother's Day in 1909. Her father, William Smart, was a Civil War veteran who raised six children on his own. Dodd's efforts led to the first Father's Day celebration in Spokane, Washington, on June 19, 1910. In 1972, Father's Day was made a permanent national holiday in the United States by President Richard Nixon.

Today, Father's Day is celebrated in many countries around the world, typically in the months of March, May, June, or September. It is a day to express appreciation and love for fathers and father figures, and to recognize the important role they play in families and communities.
