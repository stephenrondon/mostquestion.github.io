This is typically done to preserve the integrity of the original source material, even if it contains errors. For example, if a historical document contains a quote with a misspelled word, the editor may choose to include the word as it was originally written and add [sic] after it to indicate that the error is not the editor's.

Sic can also be used to highlight a particular word or phrase in a text. For example, an author may use sic to draw attention to a word that is being used in an unusual or ironic way.
