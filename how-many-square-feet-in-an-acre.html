The Curious Case of an Acre's Square Footage

An acre is a unit of land area used in the imperial system and US customary units. It is defined as the area of a rectangle that is 43,560 square feet, or 4,840 square yards. This means that there are 43,560 square feet in an acre.

The acre is a commonly used unit of measurement for land in the United States, Canada, and other countries that use the imperial system. It is often used to measure the size of farms, ranches, and other large tracts of land.

The acre has a long history, dating back to the Roman Empire. The Roman unit of land area was the jugerum, which was equivalent to about 0.62 acres. The acre was introduced to England by the Normans in the 11th century, and it has been used in the United States since the colonial era.
