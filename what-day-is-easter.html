FAQs about "What Day Is Easter?"

This section answers frequently asked questions about the date of Easter, its significance, and related topics.

Question 1: When is Easter celebrated?


Easter is celebrated on the first Sunday after the first full moon following the spring equinox, which falls around March 21st. This means that the date of Easter can vary from year to year, but it always falls between March 22nd and April 25th.

Question 2: Why does the date of Easter change every year?


The date of Easter changes every year because it is based on the lunar calendar, which is based on the cycles of the moon. The spring equinox, which is the day when the sun crosses the celestial equator moving northward, also affects the date of Easter.

Question 3: What is the significance of Easter?


Easter is the most important holiday in the Christian calendar. It commemorates the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, which is the foundation of the Christian faith. Easter is a time to celebrate new life, hope, and the victory of good over evil.

Question 4: How is Easter celebrated?


Easter is celebrated in many ways around the world. Some common traditions include attending church services, exchanging Easter eggs, and having special Easter meals with family and friends.

Question 5: What are the origins of Easter?


The origins of Easter can be traced back to ancient pagan festivals that celebrated the arrival of spring. Over time, these festivals were Christianized and became associated with the resurrection of Jesus Christ.

Question 6: What is the difference between Easter and Passover?


Easter and Passover are two separate holidays with different origins and meanings. Easter is a Christian holiday that celebrates the resurrection of Jesus Christ, while Passover is a Jewish holiday that commemorates the liberation of the Israelites from slavery in Egypt.
