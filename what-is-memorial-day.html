A Guide to Understanding Memorial Day: Honoring the Fallen

Memorial Day, originally known as Decoration Day, is a federal holiday in the United States observed annually on the last Monday in May. It commemorates the men and women who died while serving in the U.S. military.

Memorial Day is a day to remember and honor those who have made the ultimate sacrifice for our country. It is also a day to reflect on the importance of freedom and democracy, and to recommit ourselves to the values that our veterans have fought and died for.

The first Memorial Day was observed on May 30, 1868, at Arlington National Cemetery. The holiday was originally established to honor the Union soldiers who died during the Civil War, but it was later expanded to include all American service members who have died in wars.
