Question 5: What are the arguments for making Washington, D.C. a state?


Proponents of statehood argue that it would give Washington, D.C. residents full representation in Congress and a greater say in how the city is governed.

Question 6: What are the arguments against making Washington, D.C. a state?


Opponents of statehood argue that it would disrupt the balance of power between the federal government and the states. They also argue that it is unnecessary, as Washington, D.C. already has a high degree of autonomy.
