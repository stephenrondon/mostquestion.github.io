Question 2: Are canker sores contagious?


No, canker sores are not contagious and cannot be spread from person to person. They are caused by internal factors and are not transmissible through contact or sharing utensils.

Tips to Alleviate Canker Sores

Canker sores, also known as aphthous ulcers, are common oral lesions that can cause significant discomfort. Understanding effective strategies to manage canker sores is crucial for individuals seeking relief. Here are several valuable tips to consider:

Maintain meticulous oral hygiene: Regular brushing and flossing help prevent plaque accumulation and reduce the risk of developing canker sores. Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and a non-abrasive toothpaste to avoid irritating the oral mucosa.

Rinse with salt water: Gargling with warm salt water can help reduce inflammation and promote healing. Dissolve a teaspoon of salt in a cup of warm water and rinse your mouth for 30 seconds, repeating several times a day.

Apply baking soda paste: Baking soda has antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. Create a paste by mixing baking soda with a small amount of water and apply it directly to the canker sore. Leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing.

Use over-the-counter pain relievers: Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen or naproxen can reduce pain and inflammation associated with canker sores. Follow the dosage instructions carefully and avoid exceeding the recommended daily intake.

Protect canker sores from further irritation: Avoid touching or rubbing canker sores, as this can worsen discomfort and delay healing. Use a soft toothbrush and be cautious when eating or drinking to prevent further irritation.

Manage stress: Stress can trigger or exacerbate canker sores. Engage in stress-reducing activities such as exercise, yoga, or meditation to help manage stress levels and reduce the likelihood of outbreaks.

Identify and avoid trigger foods: Certain foods, such as acidic fruits, spicy dishes, and nuts, can trigger canker sores in some individuals. Identifying and avoiding potential triggers can help prevent outbreaks.

Consult a healthcare professional: If canker sores are severe, persistent, or accompanied by other symptoms such as fever or swollen lymph nodes, seek medical attention. Underlying medical conditions may require specific treatment.
