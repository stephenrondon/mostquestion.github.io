The Ultimate Guide to Resetting Your iPad: Step-by-Step Instructions

Resetting an iPad can be a useful troubleshooting step or a way to prepare the device for a new user. There are two main ways to reset an iPad: through the Settings app or through iTunes.


To reset an iPad through the Settings app:

  1. Tap the Settings icon on the iPad's home screen.
  2. Scroll down and tap on "General".
  3. Scroll down and tap on "Reset".
  4. Tap on "Erase All Content and Settings".
  5. Enter your iPad's passcode if prompted.
  6. Tap on "Erase iPad".


To reset an iPad through iTunes:

  1. Connect the iPad to your computer using a USB cable.
  2. Open iTunes on your computer.
  3. Select your iPad in the iTunes sidebar.
  4. Click on the "Summary" tab.
  5. Click on the "Restore iPad" button.
  6. Click on the "Restore" button again to confirm.

Resetting an iPad will erase all of the data on the device, so it is important to back up your data before you reset it. You can back up your data to iCloud or to your computer using iTunes.
