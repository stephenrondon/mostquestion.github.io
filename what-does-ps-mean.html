Pressure sore

A pressure sore is a type of skin damage that can occur when a person is immobile for a long period of time. Pressure sores are also known as bedsores or decubitus ulcers. They typically develop on bony areas of the body, such as the heels, elbows, and tailbone.

Pressure sores are caused by a lack of blood flow to the skin. When a person is immobile, the pressure of their body weight can cut off the blood supply to certain areas of the skin. This can lead to tissue damage and the formation of a pressure sore.

Pressure sores can be a serious health problem. They can be painful, and they can also lead to infection. In severe cases, pressure sores can even lead to death.

The term "PS" is sometimes used to refer to pressure sores. For example, healthcare professionals might use the term "PS" in a patient's medical chart to indicate that the patient has a pressure sore.
